Armaan Malik(born 22 July 1995) is an Indian singer, songwriter, record producer, voice-over, performer, dancer and actor. He is known for his singing in multiple languages, including Hindi, Telugu, English, Bengali, Kannada, Marathi, Tamil, Gujarati, Punjabi, Urdu and Malayalam. In 2006, he took part in Sa Re Ga Ma Pa L'il Champs but was eliminated after finishing in 8th position. He is the brother of music composer Amaal Mallik. Previously represented by Universal Music India and T-Series, he is now represented by Arista Records.[1][2][3] He has his own record label called Always Music Global in partnership with Warner Music India.[3] His first on-screen appearance was in the film Kaccha Limboo in 2011

------ABOUT------

Indian singer-songwriter Armaan Malik is quickly becoming one of the most popular names in Bollywood. Having begun his career at a young age, Mailk spent most of his teenage years establishing himself as an exciting prospect, recording numerous tracks on several Bollywood hit films. The son of music director Daboo Malik, Armaan signed a deal with Universal Music India in 2001, aged just 16. By that time, Malik had already sung 4 tracks on the film ‘Chillar Party’. He continued this success with songs on ‘Jai Ho’, starring Salman Khan. The turn of the decade saw Malik collaborate with more high-profile names, including Rishi Kapoor, star of ‘Kapoor & Sons’ in 2016, ‘Ki & Ka’s leads Kareena Kapoor and Arjun Kapoor and 2016’s ‘Baaghi’, alongside the Meet Bros. 2016 was Malik’s biggest year so far, which also saw him win the Filmfare Award for New Music Talent, amongst several other accolades.
